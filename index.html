Bobby Madamanchi

Teach. Build. Organize.

I'm a data science lecturer, technologist, and labor organizer at the University of Michigan. I teach data science and digital problem solving, build worker-centered tools, and organize in a time of technological change.

UMSI Faculty Page Google Scholar LinkedIn

I'm a Lecturer IV at the University of Michigan School of Information, where I teach graduate students and working professionals to think with data and build things. My courses are organized around real problems and student-designed projects.

I facilitate student-centered research across sports analytics, civic technology, educational technology, computational biology, and electoral modeling. My students have built NBA career trajectory models, real-time jumpshot form analyzers, virtual cell simulations, electoral models, and worker-centered tools.

I hold a PhD in Cancer Biology from Vanderbilt University and was the Independent Future of Work & Learning Postdoctoral Fellow at Purdue Polytechnic.

I'm the cofounder of PlanArc, a student success startup backed by Ann Arbor SPARK and UM Innovation Partnerships. PlanArc helps students with executive functioning challenges turn intentions into action. We're piloting with 500 students at the University of Michigan in Fall 2026.

My technical work includes virtual cell and tissue simulations, AI-assisted scientific workflows, worker-centered organizing tools, and models for electoral and labor analysis.

I'm a steward in LEO (AFT Local 6244). I've served on bargaining teams negotiating contracts covering thousands of lecturers and hundreds of millions of dollars.

I'm a Faculty Affiliate at the Center for Labor & Community Studies at UM-Dearborn, where I work on digital visibility for labor perspectives.

I'm interested in building political infrastructure for workers' representation and preparing the labor movement for a generative AI world.
